Lasagna recipe

Description

  • Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish made with layers of pasta, sauce, and cheese.
  • It is typically baked in the oven until golden and bubbly.
  • The most common type is made with ground meat, tomato sauce, and béchamel.
  • Vegetarian and seafood variations also exist.
  • Lasagna is often served with garlic bread and a fresh salad.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 400g ricotta cheese
  • 200g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 100g parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat, then sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  4. Add the ground beef to the pan, cooking until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  5. Stir in the marinara sauce, salt, and pepper, and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  7. In a baking dish, layer the lasagna: start with a layer of sauce, then noodles, then ricotta mixture, and repeat until all ingredients are used.
  8. Top the final layer with mozzarella and the remaining parmesan cheese.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  10. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before serving.
